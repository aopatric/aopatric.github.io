Throughout my time at MIT my interests have fallen largely within the fields of natural language processing and machine learning. I have previous experience in natural language processing, computational biology, distributed systems, computer vision, and have interest in systems programming, in particular with applications in deep learning. For more about my technical interests and background, see my CV.

Background
======
I grew up in East Los Angeles, California with my parents, 3 older sisters, and my aunt. I attended Don Bosco Technical Institute in Rosemead, CA where I mostly spent my time tinkering with consumer pc hardware. Early on in my life, I fell in love with electronics and video games. As I got older, the electronic interest became gpu interest,and the video game interest became machine learning interest. Upon arrival at MIT, I used the momentum I gained early in life to kickstart my academic career in the first few years of my undergrad. My first two years were spent taking prerequisites and introductory courses before a summer research project at MIT's Institute for Medical Engineering and Science in the Edelman Lab that involved the training, evaluation, and deployment of a MLM trained on single-cell RNA transcription data for representation learning. The learned representations were then processed for downstream tasks such as classification. From then, my focus has turned further towards the pure machine learning side of things as I have take MIT's natural language processing course as well as audited the graduate machine learning and deep learning courses. To go along with this, I've most recently joined a project at the MIT Media Lab, with the goal of creating a protocol for the training of decentralized machine learning models in a data privacy-oriented manner.

(Non-Academic) Interests
------
When I'm not working on a project, I like to both listen to and make music of varying genres that I find interesting. Over time, this has led to me picking up music production and guitar, two of my favorite things to do in my free time apart from watching basketball or spending time with loved ones. I've also gone down countless rabbit holes in my free time (cubing, comp fps, keyboards, mice, mousepads, audio equipment, vim, tmux, etc.) that I have been grateful enough to experience in the last 20 years of my life.
